Outreach and Education

An opening for a program called Engineering Ambassadors appeared before me. I had done outreach before: going back to my high school to jump-start their music program, playing music for elementary schools to convince them to pick up band or orchestra. But this was my first opportunity at STEM education. Just to join, I had to teach a lesson of any topic to a group of other students and staff. As I crafted my topic, teaching about music, I had to think about what my purpose, my audience, and what the subject matter was. Reflecting on these as I built my lesson, I began to realize that I really enjoyed teaching and educating. As I've worked as an Engineering Ambassador, I've had the chance to organize events, speak with students from all around the state, and work with other like-minded ambassadors. I'd found yet another community, and another passion, to be involved in and pursue.
