Finale

My experiences growing through UW have made me into the person I am today. The isolation of the pandemic pushed me to see the value in community and the need for meaning behind what I do in school. My time in Jazz Directory and Music for Charity showed me how to take my passions to achieve those ends, building and leading community within my peers and using those passions to meaningful ends. My struggles through coursework and my connection to my peers led me to understand the value of knowledge, no matter the subject. And my work as an Engineering Ambassador showed me the love of spreading that knowledge to those who have yet to see it. I'm continuing to work with the BCME lab to research biomedical devices; I'll be hosting a jazz concert with Music for Charity to celebrate Black History Month; and I'm continuing to do outreach with Engineering Ambassadors to spread awareness of engineering and STEM to local youth. I've taken steps to continue a path towards STEM education and community building by taking education classes and planning more outreach events beyond Engineering Ambassadors. I'm still growing, still learning, and still searching for the limits of what makes meaning in what I do. But my goal is to make sure that my future self takes what I've learned, what I've experienced, to make meaning through building up the communities around me, whether it be advancing technology though my research, STEM education, or spreading the love of music.
